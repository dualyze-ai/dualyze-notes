# How Alice Found and Merged Three Overlapping AWS Notes

Alice has been taking notes on AWS for two years.

Along the way she created `AWS SAM.md`, `AWS Serverless.md`, and `Lambda.md` — each time convinced she needed a fresh note.

Now she suspects they overlap. But reading all three to figure out what to keep would take an hour.

Bob introduces Dualyze Notes.

---

## The problem: notes that grew in parallel

---

**Alice:**
I have three notes on roughly the same AWS topic. I'm not sure what's in each of them at this point.

**Bob:**
What are they?

**Alice:**
`AWS SAM.md`, `AWS Serverless.md`, and `Lambda.md`. I think SAM and Serverless are basically the same thing. And Lambda probably overlaps with both.

**Bob:**
Open `AWS SAM.md` and right-click → **Find similar notes**.

**Alice:**
A panel opened on the right side. It's showing:

```
Similar to: AWS SAM.md

82%  Merge candidate
AWS Serverless.md
[Compare]  [Report]  [Create merge draft]

71%  Similar
Lambda.md
[Compare]  [Report]  [Create merge draft]
```

**Bob:**
82% is high. That's a merge candidate. 71% is significant overlap — worth reviewing.

**Alice:**
So it found both automatically. I didn't have to compare them manually.

> **💡 What is similarity scoring?**
> Dualyze Notes scores similarity across five dimensions: title similarity, shared headings, common tags, linked notes in common, and keyword overlap. Everything runs locally — your notes are never sent to an external service.

---

## Step 1: Compare the top match

---

**Alice:**
I clicked **Compare** on `AWS Serverless.md`. A modal opened showing both notes side by side.

On the left, `AWS SAM.md`. On the right, `AWS Serverless.md`.

**Bob:**
Look at the score breakdown at the top.

**Alice:**
```
Overall: 82%

Headings: 90%   Tags: 75%   Keywords: 80%
Links: 70%      Title: 60%
```

Headings are 90% the same. That explains why I always had trouble knowing which note to open.

**Bob:**
Scroll down to the keyword chips.

**Alice:**
There are two groups: **Common** and **Unique**.

Common: `SAM`, `CloudFormation`, `Lambda`, `deploy`, `template.yaml`, `serverless`

Unique to AWS SAM: `sam build`, `sam deploy`, `local testing`, `sam local`
Unique to AWS Serverless: `event-driven`, `SQS`, `SNS`, `async patterns`

**Bob:**
So SAM.md is focused on the CLI toolchain. Serverless.md is focused on event-driven architecture patterns. Different angles on a related topic.

**Alice:**
I had no idea. I thought they were duplicates.

**Bob:**
82% overlap, but with distinct unique content. That's exactly the situation where a merge draft is useful — not to delete one, but to create a unified note that captures both angles.

---

## Step 2: Create the merge draft

---

**Alice:**
I clicked **Create merge draft**. What happened?

**Bob:**
Open `Dualyze Merge Drafts/` in your vault.

**Alice:**
There's a new file: `AWS SAM + AWS Serverless — Merge Draft.md`

It's structured like this:

```markdown
# AWS SAM + AWS Serverless — Merge Draft

_Generated by Dualyze Notes — review before using_

## Common Content

### CloudFormation Template
...shared content from both notes...

### Lambda Integration
...shared content from both notes...

## From AWS SAM only

### Local Testing (sam local)
...unique content...

### sam build / sam deploy
...unique content...

## From AWS Serverless only

### Event-Driven Architecture
...unique content...

### SQS / SNS Async Patterns
...unique content...

---
Source: [[AWS SAM]], [[AWS Serverless]]
```

**Bob:**
The originals haven't been touched. The merge draft is a scaffold — you decide what to keep, cut, or rewrite.

**Alice:**
This would have taken me an hour to do manually. I would have had to open both notes, read through them, figure out what's shared, then write a combined version.

**Bob:**
And you probably would have missed the `sam local` section because it's buried in the middle of a long note.

**Alice:**
The unique content sections are what make this useful. I already know the common parts exist — it's the differences I need to see.

> **💡 What is a Merge Draft?**
> A Merge Draft is a scaffold document created in `Dualyze Merge Drafts/`. It separates shared content from unique content found in each note, and links back to both originals. The originals are never modified. The draft is a starting point for your own editing — not an automatic replacement.

## Step 3: Review the third note

---

**Alice:**
Back to the panel. `Lambda.md` scored 71%. Let me compare that one too.

Unique to Lambda.md: `cold start`, `execution role`, `concurrency limits`, `memory configuration`, `timeout`

These are operational details — not in either of the other two notes.

**Bob:**
So your three notes actually cover three distinct angles:
- SAM → CLI and local development toolchain
- Serverless → event-driven architecture patterns
- Lambda → runtime configuration and performance tuning

**Alice:**
They're not duplicates. They're complements. I just never organized them that way.

**Bob:**
Now you can decide: merge them into one comprehensive note, or keep them separate and add cross-links. Either way, you know what's in each one.
