# Cacio e Pepe

A minimalist Roman pasta with just three ingredients: pasta, Pecorino Romano, and black pepper. Technique matters more than the recipe.

## Ingredients

- 400g tonnarelli or spaghetti
- 150g Pecorino Romano, very finely grated
- 2 tsp coarsely ground black pepper

## Method

1. Cook pasta in lightly salted water (less salt than usual — the cheese adds plenty).
2. Toast the ground black pepper in a dry pan for 30 seconds to bloom the flavour.
3. Reserve a generous amount of pasta cooking water before draining.
4. Add a ladle of hot pasta water to the toasted pepper in the pan.
5. Add the drained pasta to the pan over low heat. Toss to coat.
6. Remove from heat. Add the finely grated Pecorino Romano in batches, tossing continuously and adding pasta water as needed to form a smooth, creamy sauce.
7. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- The cheese must be very finely grated — coarse cheese will clump instead of melting.
- Lower the heat or remove the pan completely before adding the cheese.
- Pasta water temperature is the main lever for controlling sauce consistency.
