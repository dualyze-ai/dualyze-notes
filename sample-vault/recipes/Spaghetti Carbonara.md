# Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Roman pasta made with eggs, guanciale, and Pecorino Romano. No cream.

## Ingredients

- 400g spaghetti
- 200g guanciale (or pancetta)
- 4 eggs (2 whole, 2 yolks)
- 80g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Salt for pasta water

## Method

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente.
2. Cut guanciale into small cubes. Fry in a dry pan over medium heat until crispy.
3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Pecorino Romano, and a generous amount of black pepper.
4. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water before draining.
5. Remove the pan from heat. Add the drained spaghetti to the pan with the guanciale.
6. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the spaghetti, tossing quickly.
7. Add pasta cooking water a little at a time to loosen the sauce into a creamy consistency.
8. Serve immediately with extra Pecorino Romano and black pepper.

## Tips

- Never add cream — it is not part of the authentic recipe.
- Always work off the heat when adding eggs to avoid scrambling.
- Guanciale is cured pork cheek and has more flavour than pancetta or bacon.
- The starchy pasta water is key to binding the sauce.
