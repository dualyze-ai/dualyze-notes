# Spaghetti Gricia

A classic Roman pasta made with guanciale and Pecorino Romano. Often called white Carbonara — the same dish without the eggs.

## Ingredients

- 400g spaghetti
- 200g guanciale
- 80g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Salt for pasta water

## Method

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente.
2. Cut guanciale into small cubes. Fry in a dry pan over medium heat until crispy.
3. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water before draining.
4. Remove the pan from heat. Add the drained spaghetti to the pan with the guanciale.
5. Add grated Pecorino Romano and a generous amount of black pepper, tossing quickly.
6. Add pasta cooking water a little at a time to loosen the sauce into a creamy consistency.
7. Serve immediately with extra Pecorino Romano and black pepper.

## Tips

- No eggs — this is what distinguishes Gricia from Carbonara.
- Guanciale is cured pork cheek and has more flavour than pancetta or bacon.
- The starchy pasta water is key to binding the sauce.
